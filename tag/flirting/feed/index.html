The conversation above actually happened, it was one of the first times I’ve encountered a guy trying to use “player” or “pickup artist” style techniques. I’m not sure if he was doing it right. But who cares either way, he was still inspired to pretty much be a dbag. Also, I should note that I wasn’t really trying to flirt with him, I had just been in a mood to try talking to one guy per bar I went to, and try to push our art meetup idea.

So this is the last of “The Game”/”Player’s Guide” related stuff for now (I hope). My word to the ladies out there – don’t give these guys the time of day! Don’t lose the respect you have for yourself. There are nice boys out there, they’re just a little harder to find.

*Update* – An acquaintance brought it up to me that The Game also includes some other tips, such as pretending you know palmistry to strike up a conversation with a girl or faking knowing stuff about handwriting. This stuff I don’t really mind, honestly you need some way to start up a conversation with someone in a public spot (although I doubt most girls will fall for that one, but if they’re interested in you they’ll probably play along). It’s really only the offensive/negging type ones that really get my goat. It almost seems to rely on the same principles as internet arguments rely on, that people will act like a dbag and rip into someone offensively to invoke any kind of reaction. I think the best way to deal with it is to just ignore them and walk away.
