New Brunswick Saga 16 – Amazon

So you know how, here in America (and probably most parts of the developed world), we have this thing called flirting? In Japan, it just seems like a lot of people don’t do it, especially guys. The ones that do tend to be weirdos (aka chikan, which you have to be on the chui for because they’re everywhere, judging from the amounts of road signs warning innocent girls of the local menaces). Maybe that’s why a lot of boys just sit there, waiting to be flirted with. One good example is that on Valentine’s Day in Japan, girls are expected to get gifts for their guys! And guys are not expected to anything until a month later, on White Day in March! How unfair is that? All I know it I was a boyfriend-less gal in Japan, and I have a bunch of Japanese girlfriends who had the same problem. And I realized it was stupid to wait around for some perfect guy to make the perfect moves, when you can just go out and get em yourself! I know a lot of girls (both Japanese a not) who seem to be okay with this quiet/waiting bit. Moving forward yourself might not work all the time, but there’s no fun in sitting around doing nothing. I guess the feeling of initiative in general was one big thing I learned from Study Abroad. So my advice to you: Go get ‘em girls!
